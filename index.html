Year: --------- 1898;
Location: ----- Europe and China;

The world is taking its first steps into imperialism. Europeans have realized the need of colonies and protectorates in order to support rapidly growing, industrial countries. For most of the industrial powers, the view of the outside world is one of opperotunity. Some people in China dissagreed. In the past, Europeans have converted many chinamen to Christianity. In Shandong, small societies, like the Big Swords Society, congregated to preserve their beliefs. However, this soon turned to violent action towards all things foreign. The Big Swords Society, after being called upon to fight bandits, turned on chinese christians, and burning a german church. The Germans reacted in turn, dispaching the German East Asia Squadron to Shandong. Other European countries, as well as Japan, soon followed. In fact, a french ambasador helped to pass an edict, giving christian priests power over local governent officials in domestic matters. All of these actions were seen by the small societies and conservatives as an invasion. The societies banded together to form the "Militia United in Righteousness", adopting the term militia in order to distance themselves from the martial artist sects they originated from.

Year: ---------- 1900;
Location: ----- Northern China;

In January 1900, conservatives gained a majority in the government, pressuring the Empress Dowager to support the Boxers. The Boxers swarmed north to the countryside outside of Beijing, burning churches and killing christians. Foreign powers congregated at Beijing to defend their influence.
